Report: act_report¶

This report conveys all of the insights and displays the visualizations generated by the wrangled data. Following the wrangling process, I stored, analyzed, and visualized the datasets.

Storing the Dataset¶

I saved the wrangled dataset to a CSV file named "twitter_archive_master.csv" using pd.to_csv().

Analyzing and Visualizing Data¶

The following insights and visualizations were made:

1. The most popular dog name¶

According to the above graph, Charlie was the most popular dog name, with a total of 11. With a total of 10, the names Oliver, Cooper, and Lucy were close behind.

2. Percentage of different Dog Stages¶

According to the pie chart, Pupper has the highest percentage among other dog stages with a share of 66%, while Floofer has the lowest percentage among other dog stages with a share of 2%.

3. Relationship between retweet count and favorite count¶

The above plot shows that the retweet count and favorite count have a positive correlation (0.91). This indicates that both parameters have a linear relationship.

4. Number of retweets over time¶

According to our visualization, WeRateDogs had a very successful month in terms of retweets in June of 2016, with over 7,000 retweets.
